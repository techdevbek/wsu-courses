DEFINITION:  A vector space $V$ is called the direct sum of $W_1$ and $W_2$, denoted by $W_1\bigoplus W_2$, provided
 $W_1$ and $W_2$  are subspaces of $V$ such that $W_1+W_2=V$  and $W_1\cap W_2=\{0\}$.

DEFINITION:  Let $V=W_1\bigoplus W_2$ be a vector space that is the direct sum of the subspaces $W_1$ and $W_2$.  Each element $w\in V$ can written uniquely as $w=w_1+w_2$ where $w_1\in W_1$ and  $w_2\in W_2$ (See Exercise 1).
 The function $T:V \rightarrow V$ defined by $T(w)=w_1$
  is called the projection of $V$ onto $W_1$
  along $W_2$.
\begin{enumerate}
\item  Prove that $T$ is a linear transformation.
\item  Prove that $W_1=\{x\in V | T(x)=x\}$.
\item  Prove that $R(T)=W_1$
\item  Prove that $N(T)=W_2$
\end{enumerate}



\item
Let $V$ and $W$ be finite-dimensional vector spaces and $T:V\rightarrow W$  be a linear transformation.
\begin{enumerate}
\item Prove that if dim$(V)<$ dim$(W)$, then $T$ cannot be onto.
\item Prove that if dim$(V)>$ dim$(W)$, then $T$ cannot be one-to-one.
\end{enumerate}


\item  Give an example of vector spaces $V$ and $W$ and distinct linear transformations $T$ and $U$ from $V$ to $W$ such that $N(T)=N(U)$ and $R(T)=R(U)$.

     \end{enumerate}
